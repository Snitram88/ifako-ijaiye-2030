SDG 4 • Quality Education

Future-Labs: Upgrading human capital.

Future-Labs retrofits existing community centers in the Iju and Obawole axes into modern learning hubs. The focus is not just access to training, but pathways to actual employment, entrepreneurship, and technical confidence.

  • Coding bootcamps for digital career readiness
  • Data analytics training aligned to modern business needs
  • Green-collar solar engineering for future-facing work
  • Teacher upskilling to strengthen long-term ecosystem capacity
  • Hybrid solar-powered design for resilience and uptime
SDG 8 • Decent Work

Artisan 360: Formalizing local economic value.

The informal sector powers everyday life, but it often lacks visibility, trust systems, and financial access. Artisan 360 is built to create discovery, credibility, and future financing pathways for local enterprises.

  • Verified artisan and MSME profiles
  • Ratings and reviews to build household trust
  • Neighborhood-first local service discovery
  • Digital transaction trails that support credit readiness
  • Better visibility for tailors, plumbers, electricians, and more
Governance Model

A buffer structure designed to protect delivery and trust.

The strongest institutional signal on this website is the governance logic. It shows that the project has anticipated political risk, corruption risk, and continuity risk from the outset.

In-Kind Government Role

The LGA contributes space, political legitimacy, security support, and enabling cooperation.

Operational Project Control

Optimity retains control over funds, technology, assets, reporting, implementation logic, and delivery systems.

Community Legitimacy

CDC and local stakeholders strengthen continuity, social ownership, and grassroots trust.

Why it works

The “Iron Curtain” principle protects mission-critical assets.

By ensuring that assets remain under project protection rather than political control, the Accelerator reduces the risk of administrative capture, procurement leakage, or disruption from changing leadership. This is one of the most compelling parts of the model for serious sponsors and institutional partners.

  • Funds do not pass through local treasury structures
  • Project-owned systems preserve transparency and accountability
  • Public reporting builds confidence with communities and sponsors
  • Protected operational continuity strengthens long-term credibility
